# GTM.md — Velqen

## Who Exactly Is the Target User

**Engineering Manager or CTO at a Series A startup, 15–60 employees, paying $800–3,000/month across 4+ AI subscriptions.**

More specifically: they approved the Cursor Business seats eight months ago when the team was smaller, added Claude Team when a few people asked, kept the OpenAI API contract from a prototype that shipped, and just got an invoice that was $400 more than they expected. They don't have a finance department telling them what to cut. They're the one who signs off on SaaS and they feel vaguely certain they're overpaying, but auditing it manually would take an afternoon they don't have.

They are not a "startup founder." They are technical, they have budget authority, and they feel mild guilt every time they see the monthly AI bill.

## How to Get the First 100 Users in 30 Days, $0 Paid

**Week 1 — Show HN:**
Post "Show HN: I built a free AI tool spend auditor — here's what it found for a real team" on Hacker News. The post needs to include an actual shareable audit URL (not a screenshot) of a real or realistic stack showing $400+/month in savings. HN readers click through if the tool does something immediately. Target: 40–60 audits from this single post.

**Week 1 — r/ExperiencedDevs:**
Post a thread: "Audited our 8-person team's AI subscriptions — we were paying for 3 overlapping coding tools. Here's what we found." Include the Velqen share link. Do not lead with the product. Lead with the finding. The product is how they verify it for themselves.

**Week 2 — DM the complainers:**
Search X for recent posts containing "cursor expensive", "claude bill", "openai api cost", "copilot worth it". Reply to 20 of them with a direct link to the audit. Not a pitch — a tool. "Ran this on a similar stack, found $X — might be useful."

**Week 2 — Indie Hackers Slack #tools:**
Post the audit result of your own stack or a demo stack. Ask for feedback on the recommendations. Indie hackers will run it on their stacks to check your work.

**Week 3 — Cold DM founders on X:**
Find 30 founders or EMs who have tweeted about AI tool costs in the last 60 days. DM them a pre-filled audit link with their likely stack. Not "check out my tool" — "I pre-filled this based on what you mentioned — took 2 minutes to finish, curious if the numbers match."

**Week 4 — The share loop:**
By week 3, some users will share their audit results. Every share URL is a permanent distribution moment. Optimise the share page OG image to show the savings number large and clearly so that when it gets pasted into Slack or a tweet, it creates curiosity.

## The Unfair Distribution Channel

Credex already has a list of companies that overbought AI credits. These are real businesses that purchased more than they needed — they know they overspent and are aware of the problem. A cold email from Credex saying "we built a free tool that might show you where you overpaid" is not spam — it's directly relevant. No other tool launching on Product Hunt has that list. That is the channel no competitor can replicate.

## What Week-1 Traction Looks Like If This Works

- 1 Hacker News post with 40+ points and 15+ comments
- 60–80 audits completed in the first 7 days
- 10–15 email captures
- 3–5 share links created and distributed by users (not by us)
- 1–2 inbound replies asking about Credex credits without being prompted

If week 1 produces fewer than 30 audits and zero organic shares, the top of the funnel is broken — either the HN headline didn't land or the tool didn't deliver a result worth sharing.
