# ECONOMICS.md — Velqen

## What a Converted Lead Is Worth to Credex

Credex sources surplus AI credits from companies that overforecast and resells them at a discount. The buyer pays less than retail. Credex earns the spread.

Assumptions (conservative):
- A startup converting through Velqen is currently paying $800–2,500/month on AI tools
- First credit purchase through Credex: ~$1,200 average (roughly one month's equivalent spend, bought as credits upfront)
- Credex margin on resold credits: 18–22%. Using 20%.
- Gross profit per first transaction: **$240**
- Repeat purchase rate: 60% of customers buy again within 90 days at similar volume
- Estimated 12-month LTV: 2.5 purchases × $1,200 × 20% margin = **$600 LTV per converted customer**

This is deliberately conservative. Credex's actual margin depends on source pricing, which will vary. But even at 15%, the LTV is $450 — well above what it costs to acquire through Velqen.

## CAC by Channel

**Hacker News / Reddit (organic posting)**
Time cost: 3 hours per post + monitoring. Salary equivalent at $80/hour = $240.
Expected audits per post: 60. Email capture rate: 12%. High-savings captures: 30% of those.
Qualified leads per post: 60 × 12% × 30% = **~2 qualified leads**
CAC from this channel: $240 ÷ 2 = **$120 per qualified lead**

**X/Twitter direct outreach (manual DMs)**
Time cost: 2 hours per week to identify and message 20 people.
Response rate: 15%. Audit completion from response: 50%. Email capture: 40% of those.
Leads per week: 20 × 15% × 50% × 40% = **~0.6 leads/week**
Weekly cost equivalent: $160. CAC: **~$267 per lead**

**Credex existing customer email (the unfair channel)**
Zero incremental cost — they already own the list and the relationship.
Assume 500 past buyers emailed. Open rate: 35%. Audit completion: 25% of openers. Capture rate: 50%.
Leads: 500 × 35% × 25% × 50% = **~22 qualified leads from one email**
CAC: effectively **$0** (list already paid for through prior sales).

Blended CAC across channels: roughly **$80–120 per qualified lead** when the Credex list is included.

## Conversion Rate Math: Audit → Consultation → Purchase

Three conversion events, each with a rate:

| Stage | Rate | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|
| Audit completed → email captured | 12% | Based on comparable free tool benchmarks |
| Email captured → consultation booked | 8% | High-savings audits only; strong intent signal |
| Consultation → credit purchase | 30% | Credex is solving a proven pain; buyer already knows they overspent |

**End-to-end rate:** 12% × 8% × 30% = **0.29%** of audits completed become a paying Credex customer.

To break even on Velqen's development cost (estimate: 100 hours at $80/hr = $8,000):
- Need: $8,000 ÷ $240 first-transaction GP = **34 purchases**
- Need: 34 ÷ 0.29% = **~11,700 audits completed** to recoup build cost

At 80 audits/week (achievable after a successful HN launch with organic sharing), that's **~18 months to break even on build cost** from organic alone. But if the Credex email list generates 22 leads immediately and 6–7 convert to purchases, that's $1,440–1,680 in first-month GP from zero additional spend — shortening payback to well under 6 months.

## What Would Have to Be True for $1M ARR in 18 Months

$1M ARR means $83,000/month in Credex credit revenue. At 20% margin, that requires **$416,000/month in credits sold.**

Working backwards:
- Average purchase: $1,200 → need **347 purchases/month**
- At 30% consultation → purchase rate: need **1,156 consultations/month**
- At 8% email → consultation rate: need **14,456 email captures/month**
- At 12% audit → email rate: need **~120,000 audits/month**

120,000 audits/month is 4,000/day. That's not organic social — that requires Velqen to be embedded in how teams discover their tool costs, meaning SEO traction on high-intent searches like "cursor vs copilot cost" and "claude team plan pricing," plus distribution through Credex's sales motion for every new prospect conversation.

The more realistic 18-month path is not 120,000 audits — it's a higher conversion rate through a more targeted funnel. If Credex's sales team uses Velqen as a pre-sales tool with every inbound prospect (run their audit live on a call), the consultation-to-purchase rate likely jumps to 50%+, and CAC collapses to near zero. At 50% close and $1,200 average: need 139 consultations/month, which requires 1,740 email captures/month, which requires 14,500 audits/month. That is achievable within 12 months if the HN and Reddit distribution flywheel works and Credex's sales team adopts the tool internally.

The $1M ARR number is plausible — but it depends on Credex's sales team using the tool as a conversion asset, not just on organic traffic finding it independently.
